Skin cancer is the abnormal growth of skin cells, often developing on skin exposed to the sun. The most common types are basal cell carcinoma, squamous cell carcinoma, and melanoma. Early detection and treatment are crucial for a better prognosis.

Basal Cell Carcinoma

The most common form of skin cancer, often caused by sun exposure. It rarely spreads to other parts of the body.

Squamous Cell Carcinoma

A type of skin cancer that develops in the squamous cells of the skin. It can grow and spread if not treated early.

Melanoma

The most serious type of skin cancer, developing in the melanocytes. Early detection is crucial for effective treatment.
